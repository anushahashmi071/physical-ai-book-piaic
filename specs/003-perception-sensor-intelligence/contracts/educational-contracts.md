# Educational Contracts: Perception & Sensor Intelligence

**Date**: 2026-01-21
**Feature**: 003-perception-sensor-intelligence

## Learning Objectives Contract

### Chapter 1: Perception vs Sensing Fundamentals
**Inputs**: Student with Modules 1-2 knowledge and Python basics
**Outputs**: Understanding of sensing vs perception distinction and role in humanoid robotics
**Guarantees**:
- Students will be able to explain the difference between sensing and perception
- Students will understand how perception transforms raw sensor data to meaningful information

### Chapter 2: Sensor Data Pipelines
**Inputs**: Student with perception vs sensing knowledge
**Outputs**: Understanding of data flow from sensors and preprocessing basics
**Guarantees**:
- Students can construct basic sensor data processing pipelines
- Students understand data flow from raw sensors through preprocessing steps

### Chapter 3: Vision-Based Perception
**Inputs**: Student with sensor pipeline knowledge
**Outputs**: Understanding of camera data interpretation and conceptual object/scene awareness
**Guarantees**:
- Students can process camera sensor data conceptually
- Students understand object and scene awareness principles

### Chapter 4: Depth and Range Perception
**Inputs**: Student with vision perception knowledge
**Outputs**: Understanding of LiDAR and depth sensing concepts and spatial understanding
**Guarantees**:
- Students understand LiDAR and depth sensing principles
- Students can conceptualize spatial understanding from depth data

### Chapter 5: Sensor Fusion
**Inputs**: Student with individual sensor perception knowledge
**Outputs**: Understanding of combining multiple sensors and their benefits/limitations
**Guarantees**:
- Students understand how to conceptually combine multiple sensors
- Students recognize benefits and limitations of sensor fusion

### Chapter 6: ROS 2 Perception Integration
**Inputs**: Student with sensor fusion knowledge
**Outputs**: Understanding of perception nodes, topics, and real-time constraints
**Guarantees**:
- Students understand ROS 2 perception node architecture
- Students recognize real-time constraints in perception systems

### Chapter 7: Simulation-First Testing
**Inputs**: Student with complete perception system knowledge
**Outputs**: Understanding of validating perception in simulation and sim-to-real gaps
**Guarantees**:
- Students can validate perception systems in simulation environments
- Students understand sim-to-real perception gap challenges

## Assessment Contracts

### Formative Assessments
- Each chapter includes conceptual questions to validate understanding of perception concepts
- Perception pipeline exercises to reinforce processing understanding
- Scenario-based questions to apply sensor fusion concepts

### Summative Assessments
- Students can explain perception vs sensing distinction with at least 85% accuracy
- Students can describe sensor data processing pipelines achieving 70% success rate
- Students demonstrate camera perception understanding with 75% accuracy
- Students demonstrate LiDAR perception understanding achieving 70% accuracy
- Students demonstrate sensor fusion understanding with at least 80% accuracy
- Students can describe ROS 2 perception integration successfully
- 85% of students can explain perception reliability evaluation methods
- Students can identify sim-to-real perception gap challenges with 80% accuracy
- Perception processing concepts maintain at least 10 FPS during interactive sessions
- Perception systems handle and recover from failures with 95% success rate
- Systems support up to 5 concurrent sensor streams with configurable processing
- Perception systems maintain 99% uptime with recovery from failures within 30 seconds
- Students can describe error recovery procedures and perception restoration with 90% success rate within 30 seconds
