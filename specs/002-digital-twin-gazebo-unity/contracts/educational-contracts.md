# Educational Contracts: Digital Twin (Gazebo & Unity)

**Date**: 2026-01-18
**Feature**: 002-digital-twin-gazebo-unity

## Learning Objectives Contract

### Chapter 1: Digital Twin Fundamentals
**Inputs**: Student with ROS 2 basics and Python knowledge
**Outputs**: Understanding of digital twin concept and simulation-first approach
**Guarantees**:
- Students will be able to explain digital twins using the virtual laboratory analogy
- Students will understand the relationship between simulation and physical systems

### Chapter 2: Physics Simulation Basics
**Inputs**: Student understanding digital twin fundamentals
**Outputs**: Understanding of physics modeling in simulation environments
**Guarantees**:
- Students can identify rigid body properties and their effects on simulation
- Students understand time stepping and stability requirements

### Chapter 3: Gazebo for Robotics Simulation
**Inputs**: Student understanding physics simulation concepts
**Outputs**: Ability to set up and run Gazebo simulations with humanoid robots
**Guarantees**:
- Students can configure robot models and environments in Gazebo
- Students understand Gazebo's plugin architecture and ROS 2 integration

### Chapter 4: Sensor Simulation
**Inputs**: Student understanding Gazebo simulation
**Outputs**: Understanding of virtual sensor modeling and limitations
**Guarantees**:
- Students can configure cameras, LiDAR, and IMU sensors in simulation
- Students understand noise models and sensor limitations

### Chapter 5: Unity for Visualization
**Inputs**: Student understanding sensor simulation
**Outputs**: Ability to create Unity visualization scenes
**Guarantees**:
- Students can set up Unity scenes for robot visualization
- Students understand Unity's role in the digital twin ecosystem

### Chapter 6: ROS 2 Integration
**Inputs**: Student understanding Unity visualization
**Outputs**: Ability to connect simulation environments to ROS 2
**Guarantees**:
- Students can establish communication between Gazebo and ROS 2
- Students understand data flow between simulation and control systems

### Chapter 7: Simulation-First Workflow
**Inputs**: Student understanding complete system integration
**Outputs**: Understanding of simulation-first development methodology
**Guarantees**:
- Students can apply simulation-first approach to robot development
- Students understand the limitations and benefits of digital twins

## Assessment Contracts

### Formative Assessments
- Each chapter includes conceptual questions to validate understanding
- Simulation exercises to reinforce practical skills
- Scenario-based questions to apply concepts

### Summative Assessments
- Students can explain digital twin utility with at least 80% accuracy
- Students can run basic Gazebo simulations with humanoid robots achieving 70% success rate
- Students can visualize humanoid robots in Unity with appropriate scene setup
- Students can integrate ROS 2 nodes in simulation environments successfully
- Students demonstrate understanding of simulation limitations with at least 75% accuracy
- 90% of students can create a basic Gazebo simulation with sensors
- 85% of students can properly connect ROS 2 nodes to simulation environments
- Students can identify sensor limitations and accuracy issues in simulated environments
- Simulation environments maintain at least 30 FPS during interactive sessions with up to 5 humanoid robots
- Simulation environments handle and recover gracefully from Gazebo/Unity integration failures with 95% success rate
- Simulation environments support up to 50 concurrent users with up to 10 simultaneous humanoid robot simulations per user
- Simulation environments maintain 99% uptime with recovery from failures within 5 minutes
- Students can execute error recovery procedures and restore simulation environments with 90% success rate within 2 minutes
